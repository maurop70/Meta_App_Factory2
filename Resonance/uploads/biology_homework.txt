Biology Homework - Chapter 5: Evolution

1. What is natural selection?
Natural selection is the process by which organisms with favorable traits are more likely to survive and reproduce.

2. Define homologous structures.
Homologous structures are body parts that share a common ancestry but may have different functions.

3. What are vestigial structures?
Vestigial structures are body parts that have lost their original function through evolution.

4. Explain divergent evolution.
Divergent evolution occurs when two species that share a common ancestor diverge.

5. Practice Question:
The wings of a bat and the wings of a butterfly are NOT homologous. They are analogous.
